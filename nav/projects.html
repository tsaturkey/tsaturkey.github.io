Jugend Forscht

Jugendforscht is a project competition sponsored by the Federal Ministry of Education and Research in Germany to support talented young people aged 15-21 in Mathematics, Programming, Natural Sciences and Technical fields. In the Jugend Forscht Science Competition held in Thuringia, Germany, Istanbul Lisesi students have won 1 Gold and 4 Bronze medals and achieved great successes. In the IEL Robotics Club, while the project titled "T-shirt blocking hunching" was awarded a gold medal, the projects in the field of Biology were awarded bronze medals.

Technology Student Association

Our team has participated in the competitions organized by the Technology Student Association since 2012. Our chapter was founded by our alum Doğa Doğan, who won our first trophy in the Webmaster event in 2013. We have worked diligently to achieve even greater successes ever since, representing our country as well as honoring our alumni.

MINT-Camps

MINT-EC, or Verein mathematisch-naturwissenschaftlicher Excellence-Center an Schulen, is a German nonprofit organization founded by the Confederation of German Employers' Association. It aims to inspire and encourage students in STEM subjects (MINT corresponds to STEM in Germany). Our school, Istanbul Lisesi, is one of the nearly 300 German gymnasiums worldwide (secondary education in the German education system) that are eligible for the MINT-EC certificate. This qualification allows students from our school to partake in the MINT-Camps, which have a quota of 60 students per camp, from all MINT-EC schools around the world. We are proud to announce that Zeynep Duygulu, our Sergeant-at-Arms, is among the two chosen students from our school to participate in the 2018 DNA Analysis camp.
